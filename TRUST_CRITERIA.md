# Marketplace Trust Criteria

This document defines how the platform vets, monitors, and maintains trusted third-party marketplaces
for Provenance Links. Compliance with these criteria is non-negotiable.

The platform does not host commerce.
It only ensures provenance links terminate in **safe, accountable, and reliable environments**.

---

## 1. Eligibility Requirements

A marketplace may be added to the trusted list only if it satisfies:

1. **Established Reputation**
   - Operating for a minimum verified period
   - Positive public track record
   - No major unresolved legal disputes affecting users

2. **Buyer Protection**
   - Clear refund policies
   - Dispute resolution mechanisms
   - Transparent transaction process

3. **Seller Accountability**
   - Verified seller identities
   - Traceable fulfillment and delivery
   - No anonymous or unvetted operators

4. **Legal Compliance**
   - Conforms to regional consumer protection laws
   - Tax and business regulations enforced
   - Transparent terms of service

---

## 2. Continuous Monitoring

Marketplace trust is **dynamic**. The platform must:

- Periodically review marketplaces for compliance
- Remove marketplaces that fail buyer or seller standards
- Audit provenance links to ensure redirection remains safe
- Update users if previously trusted marketplaces are delisted

Monitoring frequency: at minimum, quarterly review or after reported violations.

---

## 3. Provenance Link Restrictions

- Links may point **only to approved marketplaces**
- Links must clearly indicate an external redirect
- Links cannot hide or obfuscate the destination
- Affiliate or revenue-tracking codes are allowed only if disclosed and non-coercive

---

## 4. Delisting Criteria

A marketplace may be removed if it:

- Engages in fraud or deceptive practices
- Fails to honor buyer protection obligations
- Enables unverified or anonymous sellers
- Violates platform policies on traceability or monetization
- Suffers significant unresolved legal challenges

Upon delisting:
- Existing Provenance Links must be removed or disabled
- Users must be notified of affected links

---

## 5. Platform Responsibilities

The platform must:

- Maintain a **public or internal registry** of trusted marketplaces
- Ensure Provenance Links never point to unverified marketplaces
- Protect creators and viewers from unsafe transactions
- Avoid any financial or operational incentive that could bias trust evaluations

---

## 6. Enforcement & Auditing

Trust decisions are enforceable:
- Violations trigger immediate removal of affected links
- Repeat non-compliance by a marketplace results in permanent delisting
- Audit logs must be preserved for accountability
- Human review is required for final trust revocation decisions

---

## 7. Guiding Principle

The platform preserves reality by controlling **only the endpoints of traceable commerce**.

Marketplace trust ensures that:
- Commerce is accountable
- Users are protected
- Provenance remains meaningful

The platform does not become a marketplace itself; it enforces the integrity of the ecosystem.

---

**Conclusion:**  
Provenance is only as strong as its destination.  
By maintaining a rigorous, enforceable trust standard for marketplaces,
the platform guarantees that traceable links remain authentic, safe, and reliable over time.
