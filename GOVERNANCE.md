# Governance & Institutional Oversight

This document defines the structures, processes, and principles
that ensure the platform remains faithful to its philosophy,
definitions, monetization rules, enforcement policies, and user rights.

Governance exists to preserve **integrity over time**, not to exercise power for its own sake.

---

## 1. Governance Principles

1. **Truth First**
   - All decisions must preserve lived reality, continuity, and authenticity.
   - Monetary, political, or growth considerations may never override integrity.

2. **Non-Drift**
   - Definitions, enforcement, monetization rules, and user rights
     cannot change silently or arbitrarily.
   - All changes must be documented, reviewed, and justified.

3. **Transparency**
   - Governance decisions are auditable internally.
   - Core rules and changes are recorded in the repository for reference.

4. **Human Oversight**
   - Automation may assist, but humans have final decision authority
     for enforcement, trust evaluation, and disputes.

---

## 2. Governance Roles

| Role | Responsibility | Constraints |
|------|----------------|------------|
| **Stewards** | Maintain definitions, rules, enforcement policies, and trust criteria | Must act to preserve reality; may not profit personally from decisions |
| **Reviewers** | Audit enforcement and marketplace trust actions | Must remain independent of revenue and growth metrics |
| **Custodians** | Manage repository, archives, and system documentation | Cannot alter policies without joint approval from Stewards |

> All roles must operate **in consensus** for policy changes.  
> No single individual may unilaterally alter definitions, rights, or enforcement.

---

## 3. Decision-Making Process

1. **Proposal**
   - Any change to rules, definitions, or enforcement triggers a formal proposal.
   - Proposals must include rationale and potential impact on reality preservation.

2. **Review**
   - At least three independent Reviewers evaluate proposals.
   - Checks include compliance with:
     - Truth & originality rules
     - Monetization philosophy
     - User rights

3. **Approval**
   - Changes require **consensus of Stewards**.
   - Decisions are recorded with timestamp, participants, and rationale.

4. **Implementation**
   - Approved changes are committed to the repository.
   - All stakeholders are notified.

---

## 4. Auditing

- Quarterly audits of:
  - Provenance links
  - Marketplace trust
  - Enforcement actions
  - Monetization compliance
- Audit logs are immutable and stored with system documentation.
- Audits are conducted by independent Custodians with oversight by Stewards.

---

## 5. Conflict Resolution

- Disputes between creators, users, or internal roles are resolved by **Review Panel**.
- Panel composition:
  - Three independent Reviewers
  - One Steward as facilitator (no vote tie-break)
- Decisions are documented and archived.
- Appeals are allowed once per case, with human review only.

---

## 6. Drift Prevention

- Any silent changes to definitions, rights, or enforcement are forbidden.
- Periodic review of all core documents is mandatory.
- Repository serves as single source of truth.
- No external influence may override governance rules.

---

## 7. Accountability & Legacy

- Governance ensures that **the platform survives scale, external pressure, and time**.
- Rules are designed to endure decades, not quarters.
- Historical records of all journeys, archives, and enforcement actions remain auditable.

---

## 8. Closing Principle

> Governance is not power.  
> Governance is gravity.

It ensures that truth, continuity, and authenticity remain the foundation
of every interaction, decision, and feature.  

The platform survives because its rules **cannot drift**.
Stewards, reviewers, and custodians exist to enforce that gravity.
